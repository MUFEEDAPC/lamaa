ORTHOPEDIC SURGERY

At Lamaa Medical Tourism we understand how irritating it can be for patients struggling with bone and joint or orthopedic conditions. We completely understand how much care is essential in serving our medical tourism patients who travel to India, China, Turkey or Thailand with high expectations, that they can finally get rid of years of pain and discomfort and improve their quality of life.
1

Total Hip Replacement

Hip replacement surgeries are one of the most successful medical procedures today. It involves replacing some or all parts of the original prosthesis of the hip with new ones to alleviate debilitating pain and improve the quality of life of the patients.

2

Total Knee Replacement

Knee replacement is an alternative in extreme cases where medications and walking supports are no longer helpful. It's a procedure of resurfacing a knee that has been damaged by severe injury or arthritis, by using metal and plastic parts to cap the ends of the bone that form the knee joint and the knee cap.

3

ACL/PCL Repair

ACL/PCL surgery or reconstruction may be necessary in case conventional treatments fail. In one procedure known as Autograft, healthy muscle and tissue are taken from other areas of the body and used to repair or reconstruct the torn ligament. In case the muscle tissue is taken from a tissue bank and not from the body of the patient itself, the process is called Allograft.

4

Meniscus Repair

Meniscus repair is a procedure to repair a torn knee cartilage. This procedure is carried out on an out-patient basis as it involves minimally invasive techniques like Athroscopy that doesn't require long duration hospital stays post treatment. However post-operative protection is critical to allow fast and optimum healing and thus utmost care is recommended.

5

Arthroscopy

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive procedure that is done to determine and treat joint injuries on an out-patient basis. Also known as key-hole surgeries this process involves using endoscopic technique wherein an Athroscope is inserted into the joint using a small incision to assess and subsequently treat the damage.
